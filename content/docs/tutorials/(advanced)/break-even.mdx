**What you'll build:** the number of units where revenue exactly covers costs.

**What you need:** comfort with multiplication and division.

**Why this matters:** break-even is the first question of any business plan.

## Step 1 — Profit per unit

You sell at $25, each unit costs $15 to make.

```formula test
=25-15 // => 10
```

<TryIt formula="=25-15" />

**What just happened:** each unit sold contributes $10 toward fixed costs.

## Step 2 — The break-even point

Fixed costs are $2,000. Divide them by the $10 contribution per unit.

```formula test
=2000/(25-15) // => 200
```

<TryIt formula="=2000/(25-15)" />

**What just happened:** sell 200 units and you've exactly covered your $2,000 in fixed costs.
Unit 201 is your first profit.

## Try changing…

- Your own price, unit cost, and fixed costs.

## You learned

- Contribution per unit = price − cost.
- Break-even units = fixed costs ÷ contribution per unit.
